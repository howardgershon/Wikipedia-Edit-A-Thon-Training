The '''Utah State Library''' in [[Salt Lake City, Utah]] is a division of the [[Utah Department of Heritage and Arts]].
==History==
The Utah State Library was created in 1957 when [[George D. Clyde|Governor George D. Clyde]] appointed a ten person library commission in accordance with a recently passed state law.<ref name='50pam'>{{cite book|title=Celebrating 50 Years: Utah State Library|url=http://utah.ptfs.com/awweb/awarchive?type=file&item=10159|publisher=Utah State Library|accessdate=4 August 2015}}</ref>  The library was originally housed in the [[Utah Governor's Mansion|Governor's Mansion]] before it moved to the [[Utah State Fair|state fair grounds]] and ultimately was moved to 2150 South 300 West in Salt Lake City.<ref name='50pam' /> In 1998 it moved to its current location at 250 North 1950 West in Salt Lake City.<ref name='50pam' />
==Purpose==
According to the Utah State Library's 2014 Annual Report, the Utah State Library "provides funding, training, professional expertise and technical advice to library directors, staff, and trustees all across Utah."<ref name='annreport'> {{cite book|title=Utah State Library 2014 Annual Report|url=http://heritage.utah.gov/wp-content/uploads/USL-2014-Annual-Report.pdf?5bf229|publisher=Utah State Library|accessdate=4 August 2015}}</ref>
